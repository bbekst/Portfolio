Python | Linear Regression | Abalone Age Prediction

In this study, we employed two linear regression methods—Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) and Ordinary Least Squares (OLS)—to predict the age of abalones based on physical measurements. OLS was implemented from scratch using only NumPy and Pandas, while SGDRegressor utilized Scikit-Learn's built-in libraries. Model performances were evaluated using metrics such as mean squared error, mean absolute error, and coefficient of determination. Visualization of actual vs predicted and residual plots was accomplished using the seaborn library. The results indicate a slight superiority of the OLS method over the SGD Regressor.

Python | MLP Classification | Wine Quality

We employed a Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) to classify wine quality, implementing it in Python. During model training and testing, we experimented with three parameter combinations, adjusting the learning rate, hidden layers, and activation function. Model evaluation included diverse metrics—classification accuracy, sensitivity, specificity, harmonic mean, and logarithmic loss—calculated using Scikit-Learn. ROC curves, visualized with matplotlib, showcased nuanced performance variations across parameter combinations, yielding area under the curve values ranging from 85% to 87% in the training dataset and 81% to 85% in the test dataset.

R | Geographic Distribution of Airbnb Pricing

The purpose of this analysis was to investigate the potential relationship between geographical distribution and Airbnb pricing in Las Vegas. Statistical techniques including correlation and regression were employed. The main objective was to determine if a correlation existed between Airbnb prices and the distance from two key locations: the Las Vegas Strip and Downtown. While the initial raw data showed a moderate negative correlation between price and distance, the analysis results indicated a weak correlation between the distance from these locations and the prices of Airbnb listings.
